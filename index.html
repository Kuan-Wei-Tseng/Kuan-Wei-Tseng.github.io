ABOUT

I am a master student advised by Prof. Sato Ikuro at the Deptartment of Computer Science, Tokyo Institute of Technology. Meanwhile, I work in the Image and Vision Lab (imLab) and AI Application and Integration Lab (AI^2 Lab) at the National Taiwan University (NTU) with Prof. Chu-Song Chen and Prof. Yi-Ping Hung as a graduate research and teaching assistant. In prior to my graduate study, I served as a research associate for one year and a research assistant for for about a year and a half.
My research interests lies in the area of visual computing, focusing on 3D computer vision, deep learning, and interactive technologies.

PROJECTS

Visual-Inertial Fusion for Object Pose Tracking

Object pose estimation and tracking is to find the relative 6DoF transformation, including translation and rotation, between the object and the camera. We present a visual-inertial fusion framework for object pose estimation and tracking which supports flexible framerate and is able to perform in real-time on mobile devices.
